On January 19, 2021, Diane Marie Kennedy passed away peacefully in her Springfield home. She was comforted by the presence of her loving partner Geoffrey Roberts.

Diane was born in Springfield, Massachusetts, in 1949, the daughter of Charles Wiesel and Agnes Plourd. Her best friend was her brother Albert. Diane had a degree in computer science from the University of Massachusetts and an MBA from Marlborough College. She was also a prolific artist exhibiting under the name "Diane Shanti." She loved gardening and animals.

As a single mother and my best friend, my mom took me everywhere. We played on Cape Cod's beaches, where she taught me how to body surf and make sandcastles. She took me to college, where she studied Computer Science and Women's Studies. We lived in a small on-campus apartment. She encouraged me to fish for carp in the campus pond and play text games in the computer lab. One December, she made me a million sugar cookies and sent me to my room to read "The Lord Of The Rings" while she studied for her finals. We went to New York to see a Judy Chicago exhibition and march in protest of nuclear proliferation with Bread and Puppet theater. In 1982 She took me to work with her at Milton Bradley, where she stayed up all night programming video games on a deadline. I would play all the competitor's console games and eventually fall asleep in the snack room.

My childhood memories are very precious to me. Diane showed me how to "wing it," not stress out about the rules, and how fun it was to work hard at things you love. It's my greatest wish to share her playfulness, optimism, and enthusiasm with my children.

Diane's cremains will be interred at Oak Grove Cemetery in Springfield, under a plaque that reads "Diane Shanti Roberts." Diane will be missed by her partner Geoffrey Roberts, her son Noah Paessel, daughter-in-love Hannah, grandchildren Ada and Linden, and niece Dona Wiesel.
